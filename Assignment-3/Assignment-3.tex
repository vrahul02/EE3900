\section*{Problem Ramsey Tangent and Normal Q.20}
Find the condition that the line\\
$\myvec{l & m}\vec{x}+n=0$
should touch the circle $\quad \norm{x-\myvec{a\\b}}=r$.
\section*{Solution}
If $\vec{n}$ is the normal vector of a line, equation of that line can be written as 
\begin{align}
    \vec{n}^T\vec{x} = c 
\end{align}
Here
\begin{align}
    \vec{n} &= \myvec{l \\ m}\\
    c&=-n
\end{align}
Center of the given circle is $\vec{o}=\myvec{a\\b}$ and its radius is $r$.\\
The condition for a given line to touch a circle is: Distance of the line from the center of the circle, must be equal to its radius.\\
Formula for Distance of a line $\vec{n}^T\vec{x} = c$ from a point $\vec{x}$ is given as\\
\begin{align}
    d=\frac{\abs{\vec{n}^T\vec{x}-c}}{\quad \norm{n}}
\end{align}
\begin{align}
    r&=\frac{\abs{\vec{n}^T\vec{o}-c}}{\quad \norm{n}}\\
    &=\frac{\abs{\myvec{l&m}\myvec{a\\b}+n}}{\sqrt{l^2+m^2}}\\
    &=\frac{\abs{al+bm+n}}{\sqrt{l^2+m^2}}
\end{align}
On squaring both sides,
\begin{align}
    r^2(l^2+m^2)=(n+al+bm)^2
\end{align} 
This is the condition of tangency
